What Makes a Bleppy?

Basic Information

The Arabian Sand Boa, known by its Latin name of Eryx jayakari, is a species of snake in the Boidae family. They can grow to about 15 inches long and weigh approximately 2 pounds. The International Union for Conservation of Nature lists the Arabian Sand Boa of being "Least Concern" population-wise, and we're going to keep it that way.

Habitat

The Arabian Sand Boa can be found in desert locations in the Arabian peninsula, including Saudi Arabia, Yemen, Oman, and Kuwait, as well as in our hearts. They are burrowing snakes, usually making their homes in sand or soft soil.

Behavior

The Arabian Sand Boa is a nocturnal creature, burying itself in the sand during the day and venturing out onto the surface at night. The location of the eyes on the top of the head allow these snakes to observe the surface while still remaining buried, almost like a periscope.
